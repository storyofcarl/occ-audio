Chapter One

Rain hammered the tin roof of the old lighthouse as Alice climbed the last
of the spiral stairs, her lantern throwing long shadows against the curved
wall.

"Bob, are you up here?" Alice called out, her voice tight with worry.

Bob turned from the great lens, his face pale in the lamplight. "I saw a
ship's lights go dark," he said. "Right where the reef is."

Alice froze. "Did you signal them?"

"Twice," Bob replied, wiping salt spray from his brow. "No answer yet."

Chapter Two

The storm eased by morning, and the two of them stood on the rocks below
the lighthouse, scanning the grey water for any sign of wreckage.

"There," Alice said quietly, pointing to a dark shape half-submerged near
the reef.

Bob nodded and started down toward the tide line without another word.
